Section 01

Overview & Layout

The Pathscribe Worklist is your central hub for all active pathology cases assigned to or visible by your account. It is always the first screen you see after login and is divided into three zones:

Search Bar

A persistent global search bar sits at the very top. Use it to find any case by ID, patient name, accession number, or physician name without leaving the worklist. Results update as you type.

Header Row

Below the search bar is the header row. On the left is the page title Active Cases with a live count of cases managed. On the right are the Status Tiles (detailed below). The Total Cases tile is intentionally separated from the filter tiles by a vertical divider — it is always a summary, never a filter that hides other tiles.

Worklist Table

The main table fills the remaining viewport height and scrolls internally. Cases are grouped — critical/STAT cases appear at the top under an Urgent divider banner, followed by all other cases. The table supports virtual scrolling and loads additional rows automatically as you scroll down.

Section 02

Status Tiles & Quick Filters

The tiles in the top-right of the header serve two purposes: they give you an at-a-glance count of each category, and clicking them instantly filters the worklist to show only those cases. Click an active tile again to clear the filter and return to the full view.

💡
Total Cases is separated from the other tiles by a divider line. It always shows your full caseload and is a summary indicator only — the other tiles are the actionable filters.
⬡ Total Cases
Your full non-pool caseload. Always visible as a running summary. Click to reset any active filter.
Pool Cases
Cases sitting in a shared pool that have not yet been assigned to a specific pathologist. Clicking opens the Pool Claim workflow.
Delegated to Me
Cases that another pathologist or coordinator has explicitly routed to you for review, sign-off, or second opinion.
Critical
STAT-priority or otherwise flagged critical cases. These also appear at the very top of the table under the red Urgent divider, regardless of active filter.
In Progress
Cases with status in-progress — actively being worked on in the synoptic reporting or grossing workflow.
Needs Review
Cases where the AI has generated a synoptic draft and the report is in pending-review status, waiting for your sign-off.
Amended
Previously finalized reports that have been re-opened and modified. Requires addendum documentation before re-finalizing.
Completed Today
Reports finalized during the current calendar day. Useful for end-of-day productivity review and sign-out tracking.
Draft Added
Cases in initial draft state — received but not yet moved into active processing or grossing. Good for end-of-session triage.
Finalizing Added
Cases where microscopy is syncing or the report is in its final review step before sign-out. Bridges In Progress and Completed Today.
⚠️
Only one tile filter can be active at a time. Filters applied via the tiles interact with sorting — sort order is preserved when switching filters.

Section 04

Sorting & Multi-Sort

Every column header is clickable to sort the worklist by that field. Pathscribe supports multi-column sorting — you can apply up to three sort keys simultaneously.

How to Sort

  1. Click any column header label to set it as the primary sort. An arrow (▴ = ascending, ▾ = descending) will appear next to the label.
  2. Click the same header again to reverse the direction.
  3. Click a different header to add it as a secondary sort. The primary sort is indicated in bright cyan; secondary sorts appear in a lighter blue.
  4. To clear all sorts, use the voice command clear sort or reload the page.
ℹ️
Sort preferences are saved to localStorage under the key worklistSort and will persist across sessions until explicitly cleared.

Sort Behavior Details

Null or missing values are always sorted to the bottom regardless of direction. ISO date strings (e.g. accession date) are converted to timestamps for accurate chronological sorting. String fields use locale-aware comparison, so accented characters sort correctly.

Section 05

Flags & Priority Indicators

Flags are the primary mechanism for surfacing actionable information on a case directly in the worklist, without opening the report. There are two flag types:

Case-Level Flags (Solid border)

Attached to the overall case — e.g. a tumor board scheduling note or an oncology hold. They apply regardless of which specimen is being examined.

Specimen-Level Flags (Dashed border)

Attached to a specific specimen within the case — e.g. a pending molecular panel on one of several blocks. The dashed border visually distinguishes them from case-level flags.

Flag Colors & Meanings

Red — Critical / STAT Yellow — Caution Blue — Informational Green — Pending Result Orange — Elevated Attention Purple — Genetic / BRCA

Section 06

Row Navigation & Keyboard Shortcuts

The worklist supports rich keyboard and programmatic navigation, primarily designed for voice-assisted workflows but also usable with a standard keyboard via custom event dispatch.

Opening a Case

Click any row to open that case in the Synoptic Report view. The worklist remembers which case you were on, and when you return, the next case in the display order is automatically pre-selected — keeping you in a smooth sign-out flow.

Row Selection

A selected row is highlighted with a cyan left border and a subtle blue background. You can have only one selected row at a time. The selection follows you as you navigate with voice commands or keyboard events.

Section 07

Pool Cases

Pool cases are unassigned cases visible to a group of pathologists. They sit in a shared queue and must be claimed before they can be worked on.

Claiming a Pool Case

  1. Click the Pool Cases tile to filter the worklist to pool cases only.
  2. Click a pool case row. The Pool Claim Modal will open, showing the case summary, specimen list, and pool name.
  3. Click Accept to claim the case. It will move out of the pool and into your personal worklist immediately.
  4. Click Pass if you cannot take the case. It stays in the pool for other pathologists.
⚠️
Pool cases are hidden from the main All Cases view to reduce noise. They only appear when the Pool Cases tile is active.
Section 08

Delegated Cases

Another user can delegate a case directly to you for second opinion, sign-out assistance, or overflow coverage. These appear under the Delegated to Me tile.

Identifying Delegated Cases

Click the Delegated to Me tile to filter to only cases pending your review. The count reflects cases where your user ID is the target and the delegation status is pending. Accepted or rejected delegations no longer appear in the count.

ℹ️
Delegation count is loaded from the delegations service on page mount. If you've just been delegated a case in another session, refresh the page (PATHSCRIBE_TABLE_REFRESH) to see the updated count.

The Pathscribe worklist is fully voice-enabled via the Action Registry. When the worklist is the active view, a WORKLIST voice context is set automatically, making the following commands available hands-free:

"previous case" / "back"Moves to the previous case row.
"open case" / "open selected"Opens the highlighted case in the synoptic view.
"show critical" / "show urgent"Filters to Critical cases.
"show review"Filters to Needs Review cases.
"show completed"Filters to Completed Today cases.

"filter by [physician name]"Filters by the named submitting physician.
"sort by date"Sorts by accession date.
"sort by status"Sorts by case status.

Section 10

Common Workflows

Morning Sign-Out Triage

  1. Open the worklist. Critical cases auto-surface at the top under the red Urgent divider.
  2. Click the Critical tile to isolate STAT cases and review flags.
  3. Click the Delegated to Me tile to acknowledge any overnight delegations.
  4. Click the Needs Review tile to queue up AI drafts awaiting sign-off.
  5. Click Total Cases (or press it twice if a filter is active) to return to the full view.

High-Volume Sign-Out (Voice-Assisted)

  1. Say "sort by status" to group cases by workflow stage.
  2. Say "show review" to filter to AI draft-ready cases.
  3. Say "open case" to open the selected case.
  4. Review and approve the synoptic report, then return to the worklist. Selection automatically advances to the next case.
  5. Say "open case" again to open the next one. Repeat until the Needs Review count reaches zero.

Claiming Pool Work

  1. Click the Pool Cases tile.
  2. Review available pool cases by urgency (red urgent indicator) and accession date.
  3. Click a case to open the Pool Claim Modal.
  4. Accept the case — it immediately moves into your personal active list.
  5. Click Total Cases to return to the full view and begin working.

End-of-Day Review

  1. Click the Completed Today tile to confirm your finalized case count.
  2. Click the Finalizing tile to identify any cases still syncing that may need attention before you log off.
  3. Click the Draft tile to see any new accessions that arrived but haven't been triaged yet.
  4. Hand off any remaining critical work via the delegation feature in the Case view before logging out.
